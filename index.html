What are Lifestyle Diseases?

Here is a simple definition of lifestyle disease according to Sharma and Majumdar (2009):

Lifestyle diseases characterize those diseases whose occurrence is primarily based on the daily habits of people and are a result of an inappropriate relationship of people with their environment.

In summary, lifestyle diseases are associated with the way people live!

Read more about lifestyle diseases

Causes of Lifestyle Diseases

  1. A poor diet that is low in fibre, and high in carbohydrates, salt and sugar
  2. Not drinking enough water
  3. Not getting enough exercise or doing enough physical activity
  4. Traumatic injury or incorrect body posture
  5. Excessive stress
  6. Smoking, drinking alcohol and use of illicit drugs

Common Lifestyle Diseases, Symptoms and Behavior Modification

Life Style Disease Symptoms Behavior Modification
Obesity Body Mass Index (BMI) of 30 or higher A balanced diet with low fat content, practice healthy eating habits, exercise regulary,
Diabetes Unsual thirst, frequent urination, random weight loss, blurred vision, fatigue and weakness, slow healing sores Regulate carbohydrate intake, regular physical activity, targeted weight loss
High Blood Pressure/Hypertension No symptoms for those with blood pressure below 180/120, headaches, heartbeat palpitations for 180/120 or higher Lose extra weight, exercise regularly, eat a healthy diet low in saturated fat and cholesterol, reduce salt and sodium intake, linit alcohol, cease smoking, ease stress
